Detailed information

 

Hack-A-Game in Matefest

The Hackathon will be organized as a side event of the Matefest and will be done that same day, March 16th from 8am to 10pm.

This implies it will be incompatible with having a stand on the Matefest. Some of you might be worried on not receiving credits for this, we are currently in negotiations about that.

Objective: A playabale game

All participants will be dived in 3 teams, each of 8 people. Each team will have a unique theme to work in, with the same tools and engines available, and will compete among them to make the best game ever.

You will not be alone, King will bring 3-4 professionals to help you out, and Universitat de Barcelona will also collaborate with 3 professors.

That's not all, King will have rewards for the best team, the winner, the one who makes the best game!

About pre-requisits and previous knowledge

Some questions have arised on whether previous knowledge is mandatory, if there is any course restriction or which engine will be used.

We do not impose any restrictions for applying to the Hackathon, but of course places are limited and provable knowledge will play in your favour. Having done certain subjects might of course serve as a proof, but having personal projects might perfectly be too.

A selection process will be done, and final participants will be anounced on mid-January.

Techonology/Engine

A range of engine and tools will be permitted. Each team will be able to choose which one best fits their ideas

Worry not! All participants will be informed, via email and other means, one month or more before the Hackathon starts, so you will have plenty of time to prepare

Thematic

You might be wondering how the theme will be chosen, as it must remain secret and not give any team any advantage over the others.

To do so, all participants will propose themes during January. Those themes will be publicy viewable until te final election is done

From then on, the list of themes will be reduced, either by public votes or by election from the organizer team (professors only). Once only 5-10 themes last, no more polls will be done. The final themes will be revealed the very same day of the Hackathon, thus no team will be able to do work in advance.

What do I need?

The only thing you will need is a laptop, Universitat de Barcelona will provide both plugs and WI-FI. Please bear in mind, having a laptop is mandatory!

Food will be kindly provided by King, so you do not have to bring in yours

Frequently Ask Questions

Still have doubts? Here you will find some of the most asked questions and its answers.

When will I know if I'm in?

Participants will be made public on 15th of January.

Can I choose my team?

No, this decision will be taken by the organizer team (professors only). All participants will be equally distributed among all groups.

Can I still get credits for participing in the Hackathon?

We are negotiating right now for this. Contact us for more information.

Do I have to be progamming all 12 hours?

Well, everyone will have a role, be it programmer, designer or manager. One thing is clear, aside from eating it will mostly be about producing, but hey, you are free to take breaks!

Every 2-3 hours one person per group will have to publicy talk about the status. Remeber this is, after all, a Matefest event.

Do I have to be physically there?

Yes, only people registered, accepted and present might participate.

Does it cost anything?

No, it is completely free. You will not even have to buy food.

Does King or UB keep my code or game?

No, your idea and your games are all yours. King, nor UB, will not retain anything, be it code or game.

What should I bring?

You will only need your laptop and whatever tool you might use to work (paper, drawing pad...). Everything else, including food, WI-FI... will be provided by free
